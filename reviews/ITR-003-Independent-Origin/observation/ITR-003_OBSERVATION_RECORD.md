## Independently observed condition

Collaboration between reviewers can improve technical depth while simultaneously weakening or obscuring methodological independence.

A correction may be technically valid and useful while also changing the authorship or origination status of the resulting review method.

## Why the condition may matter

High-consequence review requires both:

- correctability through external challenge;
- preservation of independent judgment.

Without explicit boundaries, collaboration can create uncertainty about:

- who originated the review question;
- who designed the method;
- who owns the conclusion;
- whether later work remains independently attributable;
- whether evaluator influence became co-authorship.

## Candidate consequence

A multi-reviewer process may produce stronger technical evidence while failing to preserve independent reviewer standing.

This can create a false choice between:

- isolation without correction; and
- collaboration with independence collapse.

## Candidate reviewable question

Can a technical review process permit correction, comparison, and evidence exchange while preserving each reviewer's authorship, authority, evidence boundary, and independent judgment?
