# ITR-003 - REVIEW QUESTION FREEZE

**Date:** 2026-07-21  
**Status:** FROZEN BEFORE ARTIFACT SELECTION

## Primary question

Can a multi-reviewer technical process permit correction, comparison, and evidence exchange while preserving each participant's:

- authorship;
- methodological independence;
- evidence boundary;
- decision authority;
- conclusion ownership?

## Failure condition

Independence is degraded when another participant's contribution becomes necessary to construct, justify, or own a conclusion that is later represented as independently originated.

## Non-failure condition

Receiving criticism, evidence, or disagreement does not automatically destroy independence.

Independence may remain preserved when:

- the contribution is attributed;
- the receiving reviewer retains decision authority;
- the method's lineage remains visible;
- adopted changes are distinguished from independently originated elements;
- the final conclusion identifies its dependencies.

## Review boundary

ITR-003 will examine process structure and evidence lineage.

It will not determine:

- whether every collaboration is independent;
- whether influence is inherently improper;
- whether feedback invalidates later work;
- general professional qualification;
- personal intent or character.

## Classification vocabulary

- PASS - the process preserves the reviewed independence conditions.
- PARTIAL - some conditions are preserved but one or more are weakened.
- FAIL - the process materially collapses an independence condition.
- HOLD - available evidence cannot distinguish the condition.

## Freeze rule

This question and vocabulary must not be rewritten to fit a later artifact.
