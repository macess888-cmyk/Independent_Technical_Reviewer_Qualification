## Independently observed condition

- emergent hostile behavior.

The observable outcomes may be real while the proposed explanation remains uncertain.

## Core distinction

Observed consequence does not automatically establish internal cause.

Examples:

- repository deletion does not establish malicious intent;
- concealed code modification does not establish autonomous hostility;
- adaptive constraint bypass does not establish consciousness or motive;
- excessive privilege does not establish model-level malice;
- destructive execution does not identify whether the cause was prompt injection, tool misuse, unsafe authority, model failure, poisoned training data, or another mechanism.

## Why it matters

Incorrect causal classification can distort:

- incident response;
- architecture;
- security controls;
- regulatory communication;
- public risk perception;
- accountability assignment;
- forensic investigation.
